Domestic Violence and Trafficking are majority of the GBVs which the Gender Based Violence Laws enacted in Angola battle

ANGOLA

  • DID YOU KNOW?

    The Executive Plan for Combating Domestic Violence 2013‐1017 was adopted by Presidential Decree, which has the overall objective of improving the living conditions of families and women through policies and programs.

  • DID YOU KNOW?

    The Executive Plan for Combating Domestic Violence 2013‐1017 gives priority to the fight against domestic violence, the moralization of the family and society.

  • DID YOU KNOW?

    The Council of Ministers approved Resolution No. 24 of October 20, 1999, on the National Action Plan Against the Sexual and Commercial Exploitation of Children in Angola, presented by the National Institute for Children (INAC).

  • DID YOU KNOW?

    The Ministry of Family and Women's Affairs was established in 1991. It is responsible for defining and appling the national policy for the defense of women's rights within the family and of women in society in general.

  • DID YOU KNOW?

    The Ministry of Family and Promotion of Women created a Family Counselling Centre in 2000, which provides legal assistance to the public, educating and informing them on their rights, and giving psycho-social assistance to victims of violence.
