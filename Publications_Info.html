Title: Gender Based Violence and COVID-19

Publication References: Gender-Based Violence - Twin Pandemic to COVID-19


Gender based violence , which is an expression of gender inequality and toxic masculinity, is another pandemic that exists in all societies at varying degrees of prevalence and severity. It requires the same effort and attention that governments globally have given to COVID-19.

What caused the increase in GBV cases during the COVID-19 Pandemic?

The COVID-19 pandemic exposed and exacerbated existing inequalities within countries and across geographies. With half the world under lockdown as governments’ response to COVID 19, GBV increased significantly. This increase was due to the intersection of marginalization and discrimination which made certain groups of women more susceptible to GBV and COVID 19 pandemics.
These intersecting social identities of vulnerability need equal attention in order to eradicate inequality.

Title: Action to End Gender Based Violence Against Women and Children in Tanzania

Publication References: National Plan of Action to End Violence Against Women and Children in Tanzania 2017/8-2021/2


Violence against women and children is a daily reality for large number of women and children . In Tanzania its prevalence is high hence addressing it is a central development goal in its ownright and key to achieving other development outcomes for women, their families, communities and the nation.

What does the National Plan to End Violence in Tanzania intend to do?

The National Plan of Action to end Violence Against Women and Children intends to reinforce the government commitment to provide effective leadership foreliminating violence. The NPA VAWC emphasizes the actions needed for both preventing and responding to violence and recognizes that investing in violence prevention initiatives has a positive impact on inclusive growth.
Thus, available resources needs to be devoted toward ensuring that interventions to end violence in Tanzania are integrated into the NPA VAWC and accelerate the country’s progress as envisioned in the National Five Year Development.

Title: Gender-Based Violence and FGM in Tanzania

Publication References: Gender-Based Violence and Female Genital Cutting in the 2010 Tanzania Demographic and Health Survey (TDHS)


What is Know about Female Genital Mutilation(FGM) in Tanzania?

The prevalence of circumcision has not changed since the 2004 05 TDHS. Nearly three quarters of circumcisions are performed by a traditional circumciser. However, more than 9 in 10 women believe that circumcision should be stopped.

What is Know about Domestic and Sexual Violence in Tanzania?

One in ten Tanzanian women who have ever had sex report that their first sex was forced against their will.
One third of Tanzanian women age 15-49 have experienced physical violence in the past 12months. Most of these women who have experienced physical violence report that a husband, partner, or boyfriend committed the violence.

Title: Gaps in Tanzania Policies and Laws that foster Gender Based Violence

Publication References: Gaps in Policies and Laws that Perpertuate Gender Based Violence in Tanzania An Analysis and Synthesis by Human Development Trust(HDT)


What is known about the present Laws and Policies relating to GBV in Tanzania?

Some Laws and Policies were prepared in the late 1990’s to address specifically gender equality and women’s interests. Of these Laws and Policies is the;
- Sexual Offence Special Provision Act, 1998;
- the Land Act, 1999;
- the Village Land Act, 1999;
- and the Women in Development and Gender Policy, 2000.
Evidence shows that despite the existence of these Laws and Policies, gender inequality is still widespread in the country because of existing gaps and limited enforcement of the Laws and inadequate implementation of the Policies.
