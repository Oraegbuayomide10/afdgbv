Did You Know

Domestic and Sexual Violence, Early and Child marriage, Female Genital Mutilation, Sexual harassment, Trafficking are majority of the GBVs which the Gender Based Violence Laws enacted in Burkina Faso battle

BURKINA FASO

  • DID YOU KNOW?

    The National Social Action Policy 2007-2015 contains provisions relating to the protection and promotion of specific groups such as forced and or early marriages, unwanted pregnancies, violence and family and marital conflicts, social exclusion of elderly women.

  • DID YOU KNOW?

    Anyone who compels a person to marry is punished with imprisonment of six months to two years. The penalty is imprisonment of one to three years if the victim is a minor. The maximum penalty is incurred if the victim is a minor girl under the age of thirteen.

  • DID YOU KNOW?

    Anyone who contracts or promotes a marriage of a minor under the age of 13 is considered to be an accomplice, and is to be punished by imprisonment of six months to three years and a fine of 150,000 to 900,000 CFA francs.

  • DID YOU KNOW?

    Anyone who undermines the integrity of the female genital organ by total ablation, excision, infibulation, numbness or any other means. If death has resulted, the penalty is imprisonment of five to ten years.

  • DID YOU KNOW?

    Rape is punished with imprisonment of five to ten years. If the culprit is a ascendant of the person on whom the rape was committed or attempted or that he is one of those that have authority conferred on him by his function or if the rape is committed or attempted on a person particularly vulnerable by reason of his disability or over a minor or under the threat of a weapon, the penalty is imprisonment from ten to twenty years.

  • DID YOU KNOW?

    Article 422 punishes the perpetrators of sexual harassment with imprisonment of one month to three years, a fine of 50,000 CFA francs to 300,000 CFA francs and/or one of these two penalties only and, in the event of recidivism, a fine 300,000 CFA francs to 600,000 CFA francs and imprisonment from two months to five years or one of these two penalties only.

  • DID YOU KNOW?

    An amendment to the Labour Code (Act No. 033-2004/AN) in 2004 prohibited sexual harassment.
