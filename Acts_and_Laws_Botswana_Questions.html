Did You Know

Domestic and Sexual Violence, Sexual Harassment, Child Marriage are majority of the GBVs which the Gender Based Violence Laws enacted in Botswana battle

BOTSWANA

  • DID YOU KNOW?

    Under the Marriage Act it is illegal for any person under the age of 18 to marry.
    No minor below the age of 21 years may marry without the consent of parents or legal guardian.

  • DID YOU KNOW?

    The Domestic Violence Act (No. 10 of 2008) provides protection to survivors of domestic violence, especially to those subjected to Domestic Violence. The Act empowers Courts to pass a restraining order to applicants.

  • DID YOU KNOW?

    The Botswana Public Service Act, No. 14 of 2000 classifies sexual harassment of a public officer by a co-worker or by a supervisor as a misconduct and introduces appropriate penalties.

  • DID YOU KNOW?

    The Penal Code Act was amended in 1998 to make the offence of rape gender sensitive and deny bail to persons accused of the offence.

  • DID YOU KNOW?

    The Penal Code Act amendment introduced a minimum sentence of 10 years imprisonment and a mandatory HIV testing for persons convicted of rape.

  • DID YOU KNOW?

    In the case where rape was accompanied by violence, and or the rapist was unaware of their HIV+, the Penal Code Act amendment states a minimum sentence of 15 years with corporal punishment was introduced. Where the convicted person was aware of their HIV status, the minimum sentence was set at 20 years imprisonment with corporal punishment.
