Uganda

Discoveries

The Government has identified four priority areas of management of the crime of in Persons. These are:

(A) Maintenance of an effective operational coordination system;
(B) Detection, investigation and prosecution of offenders;
(C) Protection and support of victims and;
(D) Prevention of further escalation of the crime.
The partnership involves Government Agencies, International Agencies, Foreign Embassies and some local Civil Society Organizations. These partnerships have resulted into several milestones such as;
(I) Drafting a National Action Plan to combat Trafficking in Persons (TiP);
(II) Development of the National Awareness Strategy on Trafficking in Persons (TiP) prevention and Capacity building trainings of 350 stakeholder members in Investigation techniques;
(III) Identification & management of TiP victims and skills to develop counter human trafficking strategies.
