Did You Know

Domestic Violence, Stalking, Sexual Violence and Harassment, Trafficking are majority of the GBVs which the Gender Based Violence Laws enacted in Zimbabwe battle

ZIMBABWE

  • DID YOU KNOW?

    The Domestic Violence Act enacted on 26 February 2007 addresses the following forms of violence: physical, emotional, sexual, economic and emotional abuse.

  • DID YOU KNOW?

    The Domestic Violence Act provides for protection and relief to survivors of domestic violence.

  • DID YOU KNOW?

    The Domestic Violence Act criminalizes domestic violence and such acts as abuse derived from any cultural rites or practices that discriminate or degrade women. Examples include virginity testing, female genital mutilation, child marriages, forced marriages and such other practices.

  • DID YOU KNOW?

    The Sexual Offences Act (2001) protects women from sexual abuse and criminalizes marital rape and willful transmission of HIV and AIDS. The Act also prohibits trafficking of persons for purposes of prostitution and imposes stiffer penalties for violations.

  • DID YOU KNOW?

    Article 8 of the Labour Code provides that an employer or any other person, commits an unfair labour practice if, by act or omission, he demands from any employee or prospective employee any sexual favour as a condition of the recruitment for employment.

  • DID YOU KNOW?

    Article 8 of the Labour Code provides that an employer or any other person, commits an unfair labour practice if such employer engages in unwelcome sexually-determined behaviour towards any employee.
