Did You Know

Trafficking, Stalking, Sexual and Domestic Violence are majority of the GBVs which the Gender Based Violence Laws enacted in Mozambique battle

MOZAMBIQUE

  • DID YOU KNOW?

    In April 2008, the National Assembly passed legislation to punish traffickers and protect victims and witnesses of human trafficking.

  • DID YOU KNOW?

    The penalties in the Domestic Violence Act include: fines, community work, and imprisonment between 3 days to 8 years.

  • DID YOU KNOW?

    The Act on Domestic Violence states that domestic violence includes: physical abuse; sexual abuse; emotional and psychological abuse; intimidation; harassment; stalking; damage to property; any other controlling or abusive behaviour that harms or may cause harm to a person.

  • DID YOU KNOW?

    On July 9 of 2008, the government of Mozambique implemented the Law for the Prevention and Combat of Trafficking in Persons, Especially Women and Children.

  • DID YOU KNOW?

    The Human Rights League was established in 1992. One of its roles is to provide legal assistance to victims of rape.
